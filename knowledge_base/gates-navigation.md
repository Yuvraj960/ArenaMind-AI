# Stadium Gates & Entrances

## Gate Overview

| Gate | Name | Location | Capacity | Accessibility |
|------|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Gate A | North Entrance | N Sector | 5,000 | wheelchair, express, family |
| Gate B | East Entrance | E Sector | 4,000 | wheelchair, family |
| Gate C | South Entrance | S Sector | 4,500 | wheelchair, express |
| Gate D | West Entrance | W Sector | 3,500 | wheelchair |
| Gate E | VIP Entrance | Central | 2,000 | priority, assistance |
| Gate F | Accessible Entrance | SW Corner | 1,500 | wheelchair, priority, assistance |

## Your Gate

Your assigned gate is printed on your ticket. Look for the gate letter (A through F) in the top-right corner of your ticket.

To find your gate:
1. Enter the stadium via any entrance
2. Look for large illuminated signs above each gate
3. Gate names are in English and Spanish

## Gate Status

- Gate A: OPEN (slight queue at peak times)
- Gate B: OPEN (typically busiest between 16:00-18:30)
- Gate C: OPEN
- Gate D: OPEN (good alternative to Gate B)
- Gate E: VIP and ticket-holder express
- Gate F: OPEN (priority accessible entrance always open)

## Crowd Conditions

Check the ArenaMind heatmap before choosing your gate. Generally:
- Gate D and Gate F have lower congestion than Gate B
- Gate F is the safest choice for wheelchair users and those with accessibility needs
- Express lanes at Gates A and C move ~40% faster than standard lanes
